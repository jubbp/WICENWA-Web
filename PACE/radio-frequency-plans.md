## Radio Network Structure 

Radio communications operate in a hierarchical structure.

State Net Control
    |
Regional Net Control
    |
Field Stations

## Net Control Responsibilities

Net Control Stations are responsible for:

- Maintaining orderly communications
- Logging check-ins
- Prioritising traffic
- Passing messages to incident command

# WICENWA Frequency Plan

This plan provides recommended frequencies for statewide and regional communications.

Actual frequencies may vary depending on interference and propagation conditions.


## HF Statewide Net

Purpose: Statewide coordination across Western Australia.

Primary Day Frequency  
7.110 MHz LSB

Alternate Day Frequency  
7.090 MHz LSB

Night Frequency  
3.600 MHz LSB

Digital Messaging  
7.078 MHz (digital modes)

## VHF / UHF Regional Nets

Purpose: Local operational communications.

Typical structure:

Metro / Regional Repeater Net  
Primary coordination channel

Field Tactical Simplex  
Short range team communications

Example:

Perth Metro Net  
Primary Repeater

Field Tactical  
Simplex Channel

## Net Schedule

Scheduled nets ensure all stations remain informed.

Example schedule:

00 minutes past hour  
Statewide SITREP Net

30 minutes past hour  
Operational coordination net

Additional nets may be scheduled as required.

# Digital Messaging

Digital messaging allows structured message passing when voice channels are busy or unavailable.

Recommended systems:

Winlink
JS8Call
FLDIGI

## Use Cases

Digital messaging should be used for:

Situation reports

Logistics requests

Resource tracking

Long form messages

Message archiving
